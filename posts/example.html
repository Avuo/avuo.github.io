Vectors of causality Humans process the world through vectors of logic Arguments are essentially a competition of who can produce the largest number of logical vectors which lead to the outcome advocated by that person Where ignorance is belief in something despite the absence of logical vectors, naivete is belief in something due to knowledge of only one or a few logical vectors in support of the belief Reality is composed of practically infinite number of logical vectors with wildly different weights To understand and predict reality is to understand the nature of these vectors
